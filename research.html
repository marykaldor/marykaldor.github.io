Research

Current, past, and future research projects

Current Projects

Highly Variable Broad Hβ Emission Lines: I am finding broad Hβ profiles that display large changes in shape across repeat observations of the same AGN source. I am fitting these broad line profiles with a BLR model in order to tie time domain changes in the line profiles of AGN to concurrent changes in the BLR geometry on dynamical times. This work is done in progression of the Ph.D. in Astrophysics at Vanderbilt University with Dr. Jessie Runnoe.

  • Learn more

Past Projects

Broad Line AGN as SMBHB Candidates: I fit broad Hβ emission line profiles of supermassive black hole binary candidates with a single-BLR model. The goal of this work was to deduce how well a single BLR model could fit a binary candidate's line profile shape as a manner of testing an alternate explanation to binarity. Interesting degeneracies in BLR geometry parameters was further explored. This work was done in completion of the Schreyer Honors College thesis at Penn State Univeristy (B.S. Astrophysics) with Dr. Michael Eracleous.

Future Projects:

I will be describing the ways that the BLR geometry in AGN impacts the resulting broad line profile shape on a more generalized scale. This will involve studying BLR model realizations in comparison with time-domain spectroscopic data.
