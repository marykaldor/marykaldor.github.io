About Me

Some details about where I am now, where I started, and what keeps me busy outside of astronomy

Where I Am Now

Hi! I am Mary Kaldor, a 3rd year astrophysics Ph.D. candidate at Vanderbilt University. I work on understanding how shape changes in the broad emission line profiles of active galactic nuclei (AGN) may correspond to concurrent changes in the broad line region (BLR) geometry. I am currently in Nashville, TN on Vanderbilt's campus, where I am advised by Dr. Jessie Runnoe.

Where I Started

Prior to my time at Vanderbilt, I studied at Pennsylvania State University -- University Park in State College, PA where I earned a B.S. in Astronomy & Astrophysics and a B.A. in Spanish. I was a Schreyer Honors College scholar and wrote my honors thesis on AGN broad emission line variabiltiy. More specifically, I investigated the candidacy of supermassive black hole binary candidates through the lens of a single BLR model. Outside of academics, I was OPPerations Director for Penn State Homecoming, where I coordinated the largest student-run parade in the nation, a member of Alpha Kappa Psi Professional Business Fraternity and Sigma Sigma Sigma Sorority, an orientation leader for the Schreyer Honors College, and a participant in Astronomy Club.

Before Penn State, I grew up in Madison, WI. There, I developed a love for astronomy and began exploring this interest. I interned at the Wisconsin IceCube Particle Astrophysics Center (WIPAC), building muon detectors with the CosmicWatch program. I enjoyed the hands-on style of work, soldering circuit boards and wrapping scintillating material before constructing the detectors. Beyond WIPAC, I competed in swimming and soccer and enjoyed the Madison lakes and parks.
